Putter Fitting Tips

  • Length matters most: Standard putter length is 34-35 inches. If your hands hang naturally below your shoulders with eyes over the ball, that length works. If you hunch or reach, you need a different length. Get fitted
  • Stroke type determines head shape: Film your putting stroke from behind. If the putter head moves on an arc, choose a blade or heel-toe weighted putter. If it moves straight back and through, choose a face-balanced mallet
  • Grip size affects aim: Larger grips (SuperStroke 3.0+) reduce hand action and promote a shoulder-driven stroke. Thinner grips allow more feel but can introduce inconsistency. Try both
  • Test on real greens: Indoor putting mats are faster than most course greens. Always test putters on actual putting greens at various speeds before buying

Frequently Asked Questions

How important is putter fitting?

Extremely important. The putter is used more than any other club. A properly fitted putter matched to your stroke type, length, and loft can save 2-5 strokes per round according to club fitting studies.

Blade or mallet putter for beginners?

Mallet putters are better for most beginners. The larger head, higher forgiveness, and built-in alignment aids make it easier to start the ball on line and control distance.

How much should I spend on a putter?

The Cleveland Huntington Beach Soft at $149 performs within 85% of $400+ putters. For most amateur golfers, spending $150-$300 on a fitted putter provides the best value. Spending more gets you better feel and craftsmanship.

How often should I replace my putter?

Unlike drivers, putters do not lose performance over time. A well-maintained putter can last 20+ years. Replace only if your stroke changes significantly or you find a model that clearly performs better for you.

What putter does Tiger Woods use?

Tiger Woods uses a Scotty Cameron Newport 2 GSS (German Stainless Steel) that has been in his bag since 1999. It is arguably the most famous individual club in golf history.
