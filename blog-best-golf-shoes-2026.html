Spiked vs Spikeless: Which Should You Choose?

Before we dive into individual reviews, let us address the biggest question in golf footwear. The spiked vs spikeless debate has evolved significantly, and 2026 is firmly a spikeless-first market. Here is why, and when spikes still make sense.

Spikeless Shoes

  • Versatility: Wear them on course, in the clubhouse, and driving home. No changing shoes in the parking lot
  • Comfort: Generally lighter and more comfortable for walking 18 holes. Less foot fatigue over long rounds
  • Traction: Modern spikeless outsoles provide 90-95% of the grip that spikes offer on dry and lightly wet surfaces
  • Durability: No soft spikes to wear down or replace. The traction pattern lasts the life of the shoe
  • Limitation: In heavy rain, standing water, or steep wet slopes, spikeless shoes can slip. This is the one area where spikes still win

Spiked Shoes

  • Maximum traction: Unmatched grip in wet conditions, on slopes, and during aggressive swings. The cleats dig into turf for zero-slip stability
  • Confidence: Some players feel more grounded and stable with spikes. The mental advantage matters
  • Maintenance: Soft spikes wear out and need periodic replacement (every 15-20 rounds typically). Small recurring cost
  • Best for: Players who frequently play in wet climates, competitive golfers who need maximum stability, and players with aggressive lower body movements in their swing

Golf Shoe Buying Guide

Fit Is Everything

Golf shoes need to fit properly from day one. Unlike casual shoes that you can break in over weeks, golf shoes need to support lateral movement, weight transfer, and walking stability immediately. When trying on golf shoes, wear the same socks you will wear on the course and test them by mimicking your swing stance. Your heel should be locked in with zero lift, your midfoot should feel snug without pinching, and your toes should have enough room to splay naturally during the downswing.

Walking vs Riding Considerations

If you walk most of your rounds, prioritize cushioning and weight. The FootJoy Pro/SL and Nike Air Max 270G are the best walkers in our roundup. If you ride a cart for most rounds, traction and stability matter more than long-walk comfort. The Adidas Tour360 provides the most stable platform for aggressive swings.

Waterproofing Matters More Than You Think

Morning dew alone is enough to soak through non-waterproof shoes by the third hole. Even if you play in a dry climate, we strongly recommend at least water-resistant shoes. For golfers in the Pacific Northwest, UK, or anywhere it rains regularly, true waterproof shoes (FootJoy, Adidas, or ECCO) are essential.

When to Replace Golf Shoes

  • Spikeless: Replace when the traction lugs are visibly worn flat. Typically 60-80 rounds for premium shoes, 40-60 for budget options
  • Spiked: Replace soft spikes every 15-20 rounds. Replace the shoes themselves when the midsole cushioning breaks down, usually after 80-100 rounds
  • Waterproofing: If waterproof shoes start letting moisture in and you are within the warranty period, use it. Beyond warranty, waterproofing can often be refreshed with spray-on treatments
